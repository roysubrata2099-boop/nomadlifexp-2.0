Sustained structural inversions route healthy blood flow directly back to the upper body and brain. Known as the 'King of Asanas,' the headstand (Sirsasana) is one of the most powerful postures for neurological and cardiovascular reset.

## The Core Circulatory Effect

When you invert your body, gravity begins working *for* your circulatory system instead of against it. Venous blood that typically pools in the lower limbs drains effortlessly back to the heart and lungs, clearing out systemic stagnation.

This sudden rush of fresh oxygenated blood to the brain provides immediate mental clarity and enhances alertness.

## Psychological Benefits of Inverting

Beyond structural health, balancing upside down alters your mental perspective:
- **Resets the Nervous System:** Regular practice stimulates the parasympathetic nervous system, lowering your resting heart rate.
- **Forces Present Awareness:** You cannot think about your finances or scroll social media while holding a headstand. It demands total body awareness.
- **Builds Spatial Intelligence:** Inversions retrain your brain to understand balance and physical orientation in an inverted environment.

*Note: Always practice with a wall initially to preserve cervical alignment and prevent compression of the neck.*
